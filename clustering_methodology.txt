K-MEANS CLUSTERING METHODOLOGY
Premier League Player Profiling (2023/24)
==========================================


OVERVIEW
--------
This project uses K-Means clustering to group Premier League outfield players
into tactical profiles based on their per-90 performance statistics. This
document explains the original approach, the problems found, and the improved
methodology applied after analysis.


==========================================
ORIGINAL APPROACH (k=6)
==========================================

MINIMUM MINUTES FILTER
  Players with fewer than 500 minutes played were excluded.

FEATURES USED (7 total)
  - Gls_90     : Goals per 90 minutes
  - Ast_90     : Assists per 90 minutes
  - xG_90      : Expected goals per 90 (includes penalties)
  - xAG_90     : Expected assisted goals per 90
  - PrgC_90    : Progressive carries per 90
  - PrgP_90    : Progressive passes per 90
  - PrgR_90    : Progressive passes received per 90

PREPROCESSING
  Bayesian smoothing was applied to all per-90 features before scaling.
  This pulls players with very few minutes towards the league mean,
  reducing the distortion caused by small sample sizes.
  StandardScaler was then applied to normalise all features to mean=0, std=1.

PCA
  After clustering, PCA reduced the 7 features to 2 components for
  visualisation only. It was not used as input to K-Means.

NUMBER OF CLUSTERS
  k=6, chosen via the elbow method and silhouette score analysis.

CLUSTER NAMES (assigned after inspecting cluster members)
  0 - Builders (CB/DM)
  1 - Elite Creators (Wingers/CAM)
  2 - Engines (Box-to-Box / Attacking Fullbacks)
  3 - Pure Finishers (ST/CF)
  4 - Traditional Defenders
  5 - Pass Masters (Metronomes)


PROBLEMS IDENTIFIED
-------------------
After inspecting specific players, several clear misclassifications were found:

  Player              Assigned Cluster       Expected Cluster
  ------------------  ---------------------  ----------------------
  Kevin De Bruyne     Pure Finishers         Pass Masters / Creator
  Dejan Kulusevski    Pure Finishers         Winger / Engine
  Erling Haaland      Elite Creators         Pure Finisher
  Darwin Nunez        Elite Creators         Pure Finisher
  Marcus Rashford     Traditional Defenders  Attacker / Winger

ROOT CAUSES

  1. No position information used
     The algorithm had no knowledge of player positions. A forward and a
     defender with similar per-90 numbers could end up in the same cluster.
     Rashford ended up with defenders because his low progressive passing and
     low xAG in his poor 2023/24 season made his stats resemble a non-creative
     player, which is the same statistical fingerprint as many defenders.

  2. xG_90 included penalties
     Haaland took 8 penalties in 2023/24. His xG_90 of 1.03 was inflated by
     this. His non-penalty xG (npxG_90) was 0.81, a meaningful difference
     that distorted his cluster assignment.

  3. No finisher vs. creator distinction
     The feature set did not directly capture the balance between a player's
     goal threat and their chance creation. Haaland and Darwin have decent
     assists/xAG alongside their elite xG, which pulled them toward the
     "Elite Creators" cluster instead of the finisher cluster.

  4. Small sample noise (De Bruyne: 1,221 minutes)
     Despite Bayesian smoothing, De Bruyne's per-90 stats were extreme across
     every single metric due to his injury-shortened season. No cluster was
     designed for a player who is in the 90th+ percentile of everything
     simultaneously, so he was placed in the nearest centroid by chance.

  5. Cluster names are post-hoc
     Names were assigned after inspecting which players appeared in each group.
     A cluster named "Pure Finishers" describes its typical member, not every
     member. Players near the mathematical boundary between clusters can fall
     on the "wrong" side from a human intuition standpoint.


==========================================
IMPROVED APPROACH (k=7)
==========================================

MINIMUM MINUTES FILTER
  Raised from 500 to 900 minutes.
  This reduces the influence of players who appeared very briefly and whose
  per-90 stats are less representative of their true profile.
  Dataset reduced from 366 to 312 players.

FEATURES USED (9 total)
  - Gls_90           : Goals per 90 minutes
  - Ast_90           : Assists per 90 minutes
  - npxG_90          : Non-penalty expected goals per 90  [CHANGED]
  - xAG_90           : Expected assisted goals per 90
  - PrgC_90          : Progressive carries per 90
  - PrgP_90          : Progressive passes per 90
  - PrgR_90          : Progressive passes received per 90
  - finisher_ratio   : npxG_90 / xAG_90  [NEW]
  - pos_score        : Numeric encoding of player position  [NEW]

KEY CHANGES EXPLAINED

  npxG_90 replaces xG_90
    Non-penalty expected goals removes the distortion from penalty kicks.
    A player who scores many penalties appears more goal-threatening than their
    open-play output justifies. npxG reflects actual shooting quality better.

  finisher_ratio (npxG_90 / xAG_90)
    This ratio directly encodes the balance between goal threat and chance
    creation. A pure finisher has a high ratio (e.g. Haaland: 5.4). A pure
    playmaker has a low ratio (e.g. De Bruyne: 0.21). This was the single
    most important missing feature in the original approach.

    Example values:
      Erling Haaland   : 5.40  (almost exclusively a finisher)
      Darwin Nunez     : 2.52  (finisher-leaning)
      Marcus Rashford  : 2.56  (finisher-leaning)
      Dejan Kulusevski : 0.60  (creator-leaning)
      Kevin De Bruyne  : 0.21  (almost exclusively a creator)

  pos_score (position encoding)
    The Pos column from the dataset is converted to a continuous score:
      FW          = 1.00
      FW,MF       = 0.75
      MF,FW       = 0.75
      MF          = 0.50
      FW,DF       = 0.50
      DF,FW       = 0.50
      DF,MF       = 0.25
      MF,DF       = 0.25
      DF          = 0.00
    After StandardScaler, this feature contributes equally to the others.
    It anchors clusters to positional groups, preventing a forward from
    clustering with defenders purely because of similar counting stats.

PREPROCESSING
  Bayesian smoothing is applied to the 7 per-90 stats and finisher_ratio.
  The finisher_ratio is computed from the smoothed npxG_90 and xAG_90 values,
  not the raw ones, so it inherits the small-sample correction.
  pos_score is not smoothed (it is a fixed categorical value).
  StandardScaler is then applied to all 9 features.

PCA
  Recomputed from the new 9-feature space for visualisation.
  Axis meanings:
    PC1 - Attacking Activity / Final Third Volume
    PC2 - Progression and Chance Creation

NUMBER OF CLUSTERS
  Raised from k=6 to k=7.
  At k=6, Kevin De Bruyne and Bruno Fernandes both ended up in clusters that
  did not fully fit them. De Bruyne was placed in "Elite Creators" alongside
  Salah and Saka, but his statistical distance from that cluster (extreme
  PrgP, near-zero PrgC) put him visually far from the group on the PCA map.
  At k=7, a separate cluster formed naturally to capture the rarest player
  type: elite multi-dimensional attackers who both score and create at the
  highest level.

CLUSTER NAMES (k=7)
  0 - Workhorses (DM/Fullbacks)
  1 - Pass Masters
  2 - Goal Threats
  3 - Traditional Defenders
  4 - Box Strikers
  5 - Attacking Wingers
  6 - Elite Creators

OUTCOME: KEY PLAYER PLACEMENTS

  Player              Before               After
  ------------------  -------------------  --------------------
  Erling Haaland      Elite Creators       Goal Threats       (fixed)
  Darwin Nunez        Elite Creators       Goal Threats       (fixed)
  Kevin De Bruyne     Pure Finishers       Elite Creators     (fixed)
  Dejan Kulusevski    Pure Finishers       Attacking Wingers  (fixed)
  Marcus Rashford     Traditional Def.     Box Strikers       (fixed)
  Mohamed Salah       Elite Creators       Elite Creators     (unchanged, correct)
  Bukayo Saka         Elite Creators       Elite Creators     (unchanged, correct)
  Rodri               Pass Masters         Pass Masters       (unchanged, correct)
  Bruno Fernandes     Pass Masters         Pass Masters       (unchanged, borderline)

NOTE ON BRUNO FERNANDES
  Bruno Fernandes remained in Pass Masters across both k=6 and k=7.
  His xAG (0.34) and xG (0.29) are close to the Elite Creators average,
  which is why the assignment feels wrong at first glance.
  However, his progressive passing (PrgP_90 = 8.58) is nearly double the
  Elite Creators average (4.39), and his progressive carries (2.49) are less
  than half theirs (4.57). Elite Creators are players who dribble and carry
  the ball into dangerous positions as well as scoring and assisting.
  Bruno reaches dangerous positions through passing, not carrying, which
  aligns him statistically with Odegaard, Trent, and Maddison rather than
  Salah or Saka. At k=7 he remained a boundary case: his nearest statistical
  neighbours are all Pass Masters.


==========================================
LIMITATIONS
==========================================

  - The dataset contains only attacking and progression stats. There are no
    defensive metrics (tackles, interceptions, clearances, aerial duels).
    This means the "Traditional Defenders" cluster is defined by the absence
    of attacking output rather than the presence of defensive contribution.

  - K-Means produces hard cluster boundaries in a continuous statistical space.
    Players near the boundary between two clusters (like Bruno Fernandes) will
    always be assigned to one side even if they genuinely belong to both.

  - Cluster names are always approximate. They describe the typical member of
    the cluster, not a rule that applies to every player in it.

  - K-Means assumes roughly spherical clusters of similar size. Tactical
    player profiles in football do not necessarily satisfy this assumption.
    Hierarchical or density-based methods (e.g. DBSCAN) could be explored.
